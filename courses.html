Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our diving courses

What is the minimum age for diving courses?

For Discover Scuba Diving and Open Water Diver, the minimum age is 10 years old. Junior certifications are available for ages 10-14, which convert to regular certifications at age 15. Some specialty courses have different age requirements.

Do I need to know how to swim?

Yes, basic swimming skills are required. You need to be able to swim 200 meters (or 300 meters with mask, fins, and snorkel) without stopping, and float/tread water for 10 minutes. We'll assess your swimming ability at the start of the course.

How long does certification last?

PADI certifications are lifetime certifications and never expire. However, if you haven't dived in a while (6-12 months), we recommend taking a Scuba Review or ReActivate program to refresh your skills and knowledge.

What is included in the course price?

Course prices include all instruction, PADI certification fees, use of diving equipment during the course, training materials (manual, logbook), and air fills. Transportation, accommodation, and meals are not included unless specified in package deals.

Can I take the course if I have medical conditions?

Some medical conditions may prevent you from diving. All students must complete a medical questionnaire. If you answer "YES" to any questions, you'll need clearance from a physician. We can provide you with the medical form and guidance.
